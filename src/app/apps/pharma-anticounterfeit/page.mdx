<Section id="challenge">
  ## Problem

  The pharmaceutical supply chain has multiple weak points where counterfeits enter:

  1. Manufacturers ship genuine drugs but documentation can be forged downstream.
  2. Distributors and wholesalers may unknowingly (or knowingly) handle counterfeits.
  3. At the pharmacy level, there is no fast, consumer-accessible way to verify authenticity.
  4. Existing track-and-trace systems are siloed, non-interoperable, and easy to spoof.

  The solution needed to be tamper-proof, scalable to low-income markets, and provide clear ROI to pharma companies to drive adoption.
</Section>

<Section id="solution">

## Solution

<AppSolution href={appData.url}>

The system works across three integrated layers — physical authentication, digital traceability, and AI analytics.

<ul role="list" className="mt-6 space-y-8">
  <li>
    <span>
      <strong className="font-semibold text-blue-100">Layer 1 — IoT Chemical Tags:</strong> Each drug batch is embedded with a unique chemical fingerprint (nano-tag) readable by a low-cost IoT scanner. The fingerprint is tied to the batch's blockchain record at manufacture — making physical tampering detectable even if packaging is copied perfectly.
    </span>
  </li>
  <li>
    <span>
      <strong className="font-semibold text-blue-100">Layer 2 — Blockchain Traceability:</strong> Every handoff in the supply chain (Manufacturer → Distributor → Wholesaler → Pharmacy) is recorded as an immutable transaction on a permissioned blockchain. Any gap or anomaly in the chain of custody flags the batch as suspect — automatically, without human auditing.
    </span>
  </li>
  <li>
    <span>
      <strong className="font-semibold text-blue-100">Layer 3 — GenAI + SEO Analytics:</strong> A GenAI layer analyses blockchain transaction data and cross-references with adverse drug event reports to identify suspicious distribution patterns. An SEO and micro-influencer strategy (pharmacists, doctors) surfaces authentic drug information at the point of consumer search — reducing demand for counterfeit alternatives.
    </span>
  </li>
  <li>
    <span>
      <strong className="font-semibold text-blue-100">Business Case & ROI Modelling:</strong> Modelled a 10–20% reduction in counterfeit incidence post-deployment. Quantified savings from reduced product recalls, regulatory penalties, and brand liability — demonstrating a positive ROI for mid-size pharma companies within 2–3 years of adoption.
    </span>
  </li>
</ul>

</AppSolution>
